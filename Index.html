Leadership Behaviors Assessment

Most of us desire to be strong leaders. We can identify the characteristics of a good leader, but what does it mean to employ these characteristics and to act like a strong leader? This assessment will help you identify the frequency at which you exhibit actionable behaviors related to leadership in 6 categories. These categories are drawn from the Leadership Framework developed by the UTA Leadership Center. This assessment is an opportunity for you to honestly reflect on your behaviors, provides brief interpretation of your scores, then provides tips and suggestions for improving your leadership based on our Leadership Framework.

Assessment Instructions

Complete the assessment independently. This reflective guide provides opportunities to learn more about yourself and better understand how you participate in the process of leadership as it relates to practicing the six leadership pillars.

As you complete the assessment, you are encouraged to:

  • Be honest with yourself.
  • Try not to overthink or analyze the statements; respond to the statements quickly.
  • Respond to the questions based on who you are today, not who you hope to become.

Question 1 of 36
Your Leadership Pillar Scores

Each wheel shows your total for that pillar (out of 30). Your score reflects how frequently you exhibit behaviors aligned with each pillar in your day-to-day life.

Reflect: Leadership as a Skill

Leadership is a skill that can be learned, practiced, and strengthened. This assessment is a reflective guide intended to provide the opportunity for growing your capacity as a leader. As you complete the reflection, you are encouraged to think critically about how often you practice behaviors aligned with our six leadership pillars.

Reflection Instructions

Look back at the 36 statements from the assessment. For each of the 6 categories, choose:

  • One statement that represents a strength
  • One statement that represents an area of growth

The statements you select do not necessarily have to correspond to your actual scores. These are simply areas you would like to explore more and reflect on as you develop as a leader.

Example — Vision
Area of Strength: I put myself in other people’s shoes to understand their thoughts, feelings, and emotions.
Area of Growth: I establish clear and measurable group goals.
